Harsh

The designer who sees the world through a viewfinder — every pixel is a frame, every interface a story waiting to be composed.

Page 1

Two screens. One camera. Harsh doesn't just design interfaces — he photographs the world to understand how people actually see.

Page 2

He photographs everything — door handles, shadows, the way light hits a coffee cup. 'The best interfaces,' he says, 'are the ones that feel like something you've already seen.'

Page 3

'It's two pixels off.' The team groans. But two pixels later, even they can feel the difference. That's the thing about Harsh — he's right about the pixels.

Page 4

At the end of every day, Harsh reviews his shots. Not for the designs. For the people in them. The best frame is always the one nobody posed for.
