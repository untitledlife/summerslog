1. Profile

Role: Product / sprint track lead for the "Interview Intelligence" product concept. Conducted user research interviews as part of the CE sprint.

Context: Sagar's data comes from his interview with Ritam about meeting habits (analyzed in the sagar-interview-analysis page), not from a direct delegation-focused interview. His insights are inferred from his product thesis, interview style, and the product direction he chose to pursue.

Interview date: March 25, 2026 (as interviewer of Ritam).

2. Delegation Philosophy

Delegation ratio: Not directly assessed. Sagar was the interviewer, not the interviewee in the delegation-focused rounds. However, his product thesis reveals his mental model.

His product concept — a real-time AI copilot for interviewers — reveals a philosophy of augmentation over replacement. The tool sits beside the interviewer and helps, but never takes over. The Suits analogy (Harvey Specter and Mike Ross) is his explicit mental model: the AI is the brilliant associate who prepares the brief, slides notes when needed, and writes up the summary afterward.

Key positions from his product thesis:

  • The agent should be "silent by default" — only speak when genuinely useful
  • Post-interview output is where you win (retention), not during-interview features (hook)
  • Pull-based suggestions beat push-based ones in v1
  • UX researchers are the right launch ICP (high volume, structured process, painful synthesis)

3. Key Insights

  1. Identified the interviewer-side copilot gap. The entire interview copilot space is built for the interviewee (Final Round AI has 10M+ users). Nobody is building for the person asking the questions. The research tools space (Dovetail, Marvin) is entirely post-hoc. The whitespace: a real-time copilot for the interviewer.
  2. "Silent by default" is the make-or-break feature. High precision (only surface when genuinely useful) and high recall (don't miss moments that matter) is extremely hard. Every competitor that tried real-time suggestions either set the bar too low (noisy) or too high (never fires).
  3. Post-interview output is the retention driver. The 2 hours of synthesis after each interview is the pain researchers will pay to eliminate. A filled-in discussion guide with mapped quotes and cross-interview patterns is worth $50/mo to every UX researcher.
  4. The Suits analogy as product narrative. Harvey (interviewer) leads the conversation. Mike (AI) has perfect recall, surfaces the right document, never interrupts unless it matters. This communicates the value proposition instantly.
  5. Standalone beats Felix integration. The ICP (UX researchers, PMs, journalists) doesn't use Felix. A standalone tool integrating with Zoom, Google Meet, and Dovetail has a much larger addressable market.
  6. Mapped 14 competitors across 4 categories. Post-interview analysis tools (Dovetail $57.2M, Marvin, Condens, Looppanel, Grain), general meeting AI (Otter, Fireflies, Granola, Read.ai, Fellow), AI-moderated research (Outset.ai, Quals.ai), and interview copilots on the wrong side of the table (Final Round AI, LockedIn AI).

4. Pain Points

Inferred from his product thesis and interview behavior, not from a direct pain-point interview:

  • Agenda coverage tracking is manual. Interviewers don't know what they've covered until afterward. AI should detect coverage from the transcript, not require manual checkboxes.
  • Post-interview synthesis is a 2x time sink. UX researchers spend 2x the interview duration on notes, tagging, and coding.
  • Interview quality is inconsistent. PMs doing customer discovery without a research background forget to probe deeper, repeat questions already answered.
  • Real-time suggestion tools are either too noisy or too quiet. No one has solved the precision/recall tradeoff for during-interview assistance.

5. Trust Model

Dimension Sagar's Approach (inferred from product design)
Agent behavior Silent by default. Pull-based suggestions in v1. Push can come in v2 with enough data.
User control User clicks "suggest" when they want help. Agent shows agenda checklist passively. Never interrupts.
Trust building Earn trust through post-interview output quality. The structured discussion guide with mapped quotes is the trust-builder.
Failure handling Missing coverage should be flagged, not silently ignored. The "done state" (all agenda items covered) is the core UX.

6. What He'd Delegate Tonight

  • Interview prep — context from previous interviews, suggested probing questions based on research goals.
  • Agenda coverage tracking — real-time detection of which discussion guide questions were answered.
  • Post-interview structured output — filled-in discussion guide with quotes mapped to each question.
  • Cross-interview pattern detection — theme extraction across multiple sessions.

7. Dealbreakers

  • Noisy real-time suggestions that interrupt the interviewer's flow
  • Tool that takes over the interview rather than assisting
  • Post-interview output that is a transcript summary rather than a structured, filled-in discussion guide
  • No integration with existing research tools (Zoom, Google Meet, Dovetail)
