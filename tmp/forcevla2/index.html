ForceVLA2: Unleashing Hybrid Force-Position Control with Force Awareness for Contact-Rich Manipulation

Yang Li, Zhaxizhuoma, Hongru Jiang, Junjie Xia, Hongquan Zhang, Jinda Du, Yunsong Zhou, Jia Zeng, Ce Hao, Jieji Ren, Qiaojun Yu, Cewu Lu, Yu Qiao, Jiangmiao Pang
Shanghai AI Lab · Tongji · SJTU · NUS · Lumos Robotics · ECNU

arXiv:2603.15169 · Project page

The idea in one line

Current VLAs (vision-language-action models) do position control only. ForceVLA2 adds explicit force awareness and closed-loop hybrid force-position control, so robots can actually handle contact-rich tasks like wiping, pressing, and assembling without overloading or losing contact.

Why this matters

  • Existing VLAs treat force as auxiliary perception, not an active control signal
  • Position-only control fails at contact-rich tasks: arm overload, unstable contact, can't adapt to perturbations
  • Humans use force + vision + language hierarchically. Robots should too.
  • Control theory shows position-only has controllability index = 0.5 (half the state space unreachable)

Architecture

Dual-level design inspired by human sensorimotor control:

Multi-view images + Task prompt + Force prompt
→
VLM (PaliGemma / SigLIP)
→
Fused contextual tokens E
Long-horizon force awareness via prompting
EE 6D pose + 6D force/torque
→
Multimodal Encoder (cross-attention)
→
Cross-Scale MoE
→
Flow Matching Policy
Short-horizon reactive force-to-control loop + force bypass
  • Long-horizon: Force prompts encode current subtask + physical context as text, fed to VLM alongside vision. Tasks decompose into 3-5 subtasks (like a state machine).
  • Short-horizon: Raw force/torque bypasses VLM entirely, goes straight to MoE for reactive control. Preserves gradient fidelity for rapid feedback.
  • Cross-Scale MoE: Three modality-specific experts (visual, state, force). Dynamic gating autonomously emphasizes vision during free-space motion, force during contact.
  • Output: Flow matching generates hybrid actions: position change + target force + subtask progress indicator.

Key formulations

Action output is a hybrid force-position command:

at = [Δpt ; ft],   Δpt ∈ ℜ7,   ft ∈ ℜ6,   st ∈ [0,1]

MoE routing across three experts:

EMoE = Σm∈{V,S,F} wm · Expertm(Econd)

Subtask transition modeled probabilistically (Beta-Exponential-Uniform joint):

ŝt = P(Θ ≤ θ, L ≥ l, F ≤ f) = [Γ(α+1) / αΓ(α)] · θα · e-λl · (f-n)/(m-n)

α=2, λ=2. Orientation, distance-to-goal, and contact force jointly determine when to advance to next subtask.

ForceVLA2-Dataset

1,000 trajectories · ~500K timesteps · 5 contact-rich tasks

  • Tasks: press bottle, clean vase, clean board, retrieve plate, assemble gears
  • Collection: Force-feedback GELLO teleoperation on Flexiv Rizon 4s (7-DOF) with DH Robotics AG-95 gripper
  • Sensors: 3 RGB cameras (2x RealSense D455 + 1x wrist D435) + 6D force/torque at 300 Hz
  • Annotations: Each task segmented into 3-5 subtasks based on force signal transitions
  • Skill distribution: Explore (45.6%), Wipe, Push, Grasp, Rotate
  • First dataset with both force prompts for task decomposition AND force-control supervision

Key insights

  • Naive force concatenation hurts. π0 w/ Force (17%) is worse than π0 alone (18%). Force as "just another input" becomes noise.
  • Force needs its own pathway. Bypassing VLM fusion for raw force preserves gradient fidelity for fast reactive control.
  • MoE learns modality scheduling. Visual expert dominates free-space; force expert dominates contact phases. No manual switching needed.
  • Subtask transitions are probabilistic. Joint Beta-Exponential-Uniform model over orientation, distance, and force. More principled than binary thresholds.
  • Perturbation robustness. When the platform is suddenly lowered during pressing, ForceVLA2 adapts in real-time. Position-only VLAs lose contact.

Training details

  • 8x A100 GPUs, 30K steps, ~10 hours
  • Batch size 32, AdamW, cosine LR decay, EMA 0.99
  • Inference: 15 Hz on single A100, action chunk size 30
  • VLM backbone: PaliGemma (SigLIP vision encoder)
  • Action head: flow matching (conditional flow ODE)

Why it's interesting

The force bypass is the real insight here. Everyone's been asking "how do we fuse more modalities into VLAs" but this paper shows that some signals need to skip the high-level fusion entirely. Force at 300 Hz doesn't belong in a language model's attention layers. It belongs in a reactive loop with direct gradient access.

The control-theoretic framing is also clean: position-only control literally can't reach half the state space (controllability index 0.5). Hybrid force-position is necessary, not optional.

The MoE learning to schedule modalities autonomously (vision in free-space, force at contact) is the kind of emergent behavior that makes the architecture feel right.
