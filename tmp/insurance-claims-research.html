Vehicle Insurance Claims

CE Sprint Research Consolidation — March 26, 2026

Executive Summary

  • The Rs 50K surveyor threshold is the regulatory cliff. Claims below Rs 50,000 are fully digitizable today (IRDAI permits app/AI assessment). Above 50K, a licensed physical surveyor is mandated by IRDAI regulation. This single threshold determines where an AI agent can own the process end-to-end vs. where it can only assist.
  • MACT proceedings are the hardest blocker. Third-party injury/death claims go through Motor Accident Claims Tribunals that average 1-5+ years (sometimes 10+). Cross-examination requires physical court presence. Bihar piloted virtual hearings but it has not scaled nationally. No AI agent bypasses this.
  • The client operates an 8-system vehicle lifecycle pipeline, not 8 separate tools. CRM, loan tracking (Equify), inspection, yard management, accounting, expenses, gate pass, and document generation are deeply interconnected. A cross-system agent (especially a Loan Lifecycle Agent spanning CRM + Equify + Accounting + Inspection) eliminates 1,500-2,000 manual handoffs per month on 100 files.
  • Theft claims are the longest and most painful flow -- 3 to 12 months end-to-end, bottlenecked by police Non-Traceable Certificates (60-180 days) and 15+ required documents including RTO forms, subrogation deeds on stamp paper, and all vehicle keys.
  • Most "in-person" interactions are legacy friction, not legal requirements. Document submission, claim disputes, agent/broker mediation, and RTO paperwork can all be done electronically today. The truly irreducible physical touchpoints are: vehicle logistics (towing/drop-off/pickup), medical treatment, MACT court appearances, and surveyor inspections above 50K.
  • Sprint 1 should demo Equify Intelligence and Accounting Reconciliation agents. These are high-impact, high-feasibility, single-system wins that prove the concept before tackling cross-system orchestration in Sprint 2.

Indian Vehicle Insurance Claim Process

Key Terminology

  • IDV (Insured Declared Value) -- Maximum payout for total loss/theft. Manufacturer price minus age-based depreciation.
  • NCB (No Claim Bonus) -- 20-50% premium discount for claim-free years. Lost when a claim is filed.
  • Deductible/Excess -- Mandatory amount borne by policyholder per claim (compulsory + voluntary).
  • Surveyor -- IRDAI-licensed professional who inspects damage and assesses claim value.
  • Subrogation -- Transfer of recovery rights to insurer to recoup costs from the at-fault party.
Own Damage Claim Flow (Accident, No Third Party)
  1. At the Scene (Immediately) -- Do NOT move the vehicle. Photograph all damage, surrounding area, registration plate. Note date, time, location, circumstances.
  2. Intimate the Insurer (Within 24-48 hrs) -- Call, app, or email. Provide policy number, registration number, incident details, photos. Receive a claim reference number. Documents needed: policy copy, driving license, RC.
  3. FIR / Police Report (If Required) -- Minor own damage (dents, scratches): generally not mandatory. Major accidents, riots, flood, fire, or injury involvement: FIR is required. Some insurers require FIR regardless -- check policy terms.
  4. Take Vehicle to Garage -- Cashless: network garage. Reimbursement: any garage. Do NOT begin repairs before surveyor inspection -- this is the #1 reason for claim rejection.
  5. Surveyor Inspection -- Appointed within 24 hours (IRDAI mandate). Below Rs 50K: survey not mandatory, can process via photos/video. Above Rs 50K: licensed surveyor mandatory. Report due within 15 days.
  6. Claim Approval -- Insurer reviews surveyor report, decides within 7 days (IRDAI mandate). Sends approval with sanctioned amount, or requests additional documents.
  7. Repairs -- Cashless: insurer settles with garage, policyholder pays deductible + depreciation on parts (unless zero-dep cover). Reimbursement: policyholder pays full amount, collects original bills.
  8. Settlement -- Cashless: funds to garage, collect vehicle. Reimbursement: submit original bills, insurer reimburses after verification.

Third-Party Claim Flow (Accident Involving Another Vehicle/Person)

Third-party claims are fundamentally different. They involve legal liability and often the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (MACT).

If You Caused Damage to Someone Else

  1. At the Scene -- Stop the vehicle. Help the injured. Call ambulance (108). Do NOT flee (hit-and-run is criminal under MV Act s.161-162). Exchange details, photograph everything, note witnesses.
  2. File FIR (Mandatory) -- Required for all accidents with injury, death, or third-party property damage. Must report within 24 hours (MV Act s.134). SHO submits Accident Information Report to MACT within 30 days.
  3. Medico-Legal Case (If Injuries) -- Hospital creates MLC record. Doctor reports to police. MLC documentation becomes critical MACT evidence.
  4. Intimate Your Insurer -- Provide FIR copy, accident details, third-party details, photos.
  5. Third Party Files MACT Claim -- Filed under MV Act s.166 (fault-based) or s.163A (no-fault, structured formula). Deadline: 6 months from accident (tribunals often accept late filings). Jurisdiction: where accident occurred, where claimant resides, or where insurer has office.
  6. MACT Proceedings -- Notices to vehicle owner, driver, insurer. Both sides present evidence, cross-examine witnesses. Proceedings take 1-5+ years in practice.
  7. MACT Award -- Tribunal determines compensation (medical expenses, income loss, pain/suffering, disability, funeral expenses). Insurer must comply within 30 days. Death cases use multiplier method based on age and income.

If Someone Else Damaged Your Vehicle

  • Claim on your own comprehensive policy for vehicle repairs (OD portion).
  • For injury/additional losses, file third-party claim against at-fault party's insurer via MACT.
  • Your insurer may exercise subrogation rights.
Practical reality: Cross-claiming against the other party's insurance for vehicle damage is complex in India. Most people claim on their own OD policy and lose NCB. MACT is primarily used for injury/death compensation.
Theft Claim Flow (Total Loss)

Theft claims involve the most paperwork and longest timelines of any claim type.

  1. File FIR (Immediately, Mandatory) -- At the police station with jurisdiction over theft location. Provide vehicle details, chassis/engine numbers, last seen location and time. Get a copy of the FIR.
  2. Intimate Insurer (Within 24 hours) -- Policy number, vehicle details, FIR number, theft circumstances. Get claim reference number.
  3. Inform RTO (Mandatory under MV Act) -- Submit Forms 28, 29, 30. Later submit RTO transfer papers (Forms 26, 28, 29, 30, 35) transferring RC to insurer.
  4. Police Investigation (30-90+ days) -- Police attempt to trace vehicle. If not recovered, issue Non-Traceable Certificate (NTC) / Final Report. Major bottleneck: NTC typically takes 60-180 days.
  5. Compile Documents -- 15 required documents (see table below).
  6. Submit to Insurer -- Complete set submitted; insurer verifies and may conduct own investigation (CCTV, circumstances verification).
  7. Settlement -- Payout = IDV minus deductibles. If vehicle on loan, payout goes to financier first.

Why Delays Happen

  1. Document churn -- Insurer asks for documents one at a time, resetting the clock each time.
  2. Surveyor bottleneck -- Limited licensed surveyors in some areas.
  3. Internal approval chains -- Multiple levels for higher-value claims.
  4. Fraud investigation -- Pauses the clock entirely.
  5. Garage-surveyor negotiation -- Back-and-forth on repair estimates.
  6. Police delays -- NTC for theft cases routinely delayed by months.

Hard Blockers

The Rs 50K Surveyor Threshold
Claims below Rs 50,000 do not require a licensed surveyor (IRDAI Master Circular 2024). Digit Insurance pioneered smartphone self-inspection with CV-based damage assessment. Acko offers instant settlements for minor damage via app. Above Rs 50,000, a physical licensed surveyor is IRDAI-mandated -- no digital workaround exists without regulatory change.
MACT: The Decade-Long Blocker
Motor Accident Claims Tribunals handle all third-party injury/death claims. The 2019 MV Amendment automatically converts police accident reports into claim petitions. Proceedings average 1-5 years, with Delhi HC data showing cases from 2013/2015 still reaching judgment in 2025. Cross-examination requires physical presence. Bihar's statewide online MACT portal (upheld by Patna HC in 2025) is promising but has not scaled nationally.

The Irreducible Physical Touchpoints

  1. MACT court appearances -- legally mandated, cases last 10+ years.
  2. Physical surveyor inspection (>50K) -- IRDAI regulation, requires regulatory change.
  3. Vehicle logistics -- towing, drop-off, pickup are inherently physical.
  4. Medical treatment and examination -- inherently physical.
  5. FIR signing within 3 days -- BNSS 2023 allows e-FIR but kept the physical signature requirement.

Legacy Friction (In-Person by Habit, Not Law)

  1. Document submission to insurer -- every digital insurer accepts online uploads.
  2. Claim dispute escalation -- ombudsman is online, consumer court has e-filing, but branches visited because phone/email gets ignored.
  3. Agent-mediated interactions -- entirely optional.
  4. RTO paperwork -- Parivahan portal exists, many states allow online processing.
  5. FIR filing (where e-FIR portals exist) -- legally permitted electronically since BNSS 2023.

Key Digitization Enablers Already in Place

  • BNSS 2023 Section 173 -- Electronic FIR + Zero FIR at any station.
  • IRDAI Master Circular 2024 -- Sub-50K exempt from surveyor; app/AI assessment permitted.
  • MV Amendment Act 2019 -- Digital documents accepted (DigiLocker/mParivahan); golden hour cashless treatment; automatic DAR-to-MACT conversion.
  • Bima Sugam (launching 2025-2026) -- Unified digital insurance marketplace with claims, servicing, unique Bima Pehchaan ID.
  • Digit/Acko innovations -- Self-inspection via video, CV-based damage assessment, instant payout for minor claims.
  • Insurance Ombudsman -- Fully online complaint and resolution at cioins.co.in.

Client Pipeline Analysis

The client operates an auto finance and yard management business with eight interconnected systems. The key insight: this is not eight separate systems -- it is a single vehicle lifecycle pipeline from customer acquisition (CRM) through financing (Equify) through physical custody (yard, gate pass, inspection) through accounting (bank statements, invoices, expenses).

The 8 Systems

1. CRM (Recently Rebuilt) Medium
  • Lead scoring with financial context -- Connect CRM customer data with Equify loan viability. Pre-qualify leads silently based on actual financing probability, not just intent.
  • Follow-up intelligence -- Monitor Equify for bank status changes, trigger CRM follow-ups. "Bank X approved the file -- call the customer now."
  • Conversation summarization -- Extract structured data from call notes/recordings: customer wants, budget, vehicles discussed, objections. Feed into both CRM and Equify.
2. Equify -- Loan Process Tracking High

Described as "post office tracking" for finance files. The tracking is built. Agent opportunity is in what happens around it.

  • Bank communication drafting -- Each bank has own format preferences and doc requirements. Agent drafts bank-specific communications, tracks response times, flags stalling files.
  • Documentation gap detection -- Before file goes to bank: "HDFC wants X, ICICI wants Y, you're missing Z." Catches gaps before bank rejection.
  • Status prediction -- "Files with this profile at Bank X typically take 12 days. This one has been sitting for 18. Escalation recommended."
  • Bank selection optimization -- Recommend which banks to submit to (and in what order) based on historical approval rates for similar profiles.

Agent behavior: Morning briefing: "5 files need follow-up. 2 overdue. 1 at SBI missing Form 16." Auto-drafts follow-up messages. Flags anomalies. Maintains bank-specific knowledge base.

3. Internal Documentation + PDF Generation Medium
  • Intelligent template selection -- Determines correct template from context (loan type, bank, vehicle, customer profile) and pre-fills.
  • Document verification -- Checks data consistency across systems before generating. "Loan amount in Equify doesn't match invoice amount."
  • Multi-format output -- One input generates PDF for bank, email summary for customer, one-line entry for accounts log.
4. Accounting System (Bank Statement Ingestion) High
  • Automatic reconciliation -- Match bank entries against invoices, disbursements, payments, expenses. Flag unmatched entries. Continuous, not batch.
  • Anomaly detection -- "EMI bounced for second consecutive month." "Vendor paid twice for same invoice." "Unknown bank charge."
  • Cash flow forecasting -- Project 7/14/30-day cash flow from Equify disbursement data, CRM deal pipeline, historical payment patterns.
  • GST and compliance -- Categorize transactions for GST filing, flag TDS items, generate CA-ready period-end summaries.

Agent behavior: "47 of 52 entries matched. 3 need review. 2 flagged as duplicates." Proactive EMI collection alerts. Monthly GST summary.

5. Vehicle Inspection High
  • Computer vision damage assessment -- Inspector takes photos, agent identifies damage, estimates repair costs, flags condition/valuation discrepancies. (Tractable is a $1B company doing exactly this.)
  • Report generation -- From photos + minimal checklist: full inspection report. Customer-facing clean version + internal detailed version.
  • Historical comparison -- "New damage on rear bumper since last inspection on [date]."
  • Valuation integration -- "Condition: 7/10. Market value for model/year/condition: Rs X-Y lakhs. Declared: Rs Z lakhs."
6. Employee Expense Management Low
  • Receipt OCR + auto-categorization (photo to pre-filled expense in 5 seconds).
  • Policy enforcement before submission (limits, duplicates, category checks).
  • Anomaly flagging ("Fuel expenses 3x average for role/location").
7. Gate Pass System Low-Med
  • Identity verification against CRM/vendor/employee records.
  • Automated scheduling ("Vehicle X inspection at 2 PM -- generate gate pass, notify inspector").
  • Audit trail intelligence ("Vehicle entered 4 times this month with no active loan file").
8. Yard Management (Being Productized) High

Being sold externally -- AI intelligence is the product differentiator. Any developer can build parking management. An AI-powered custody platform is a different category.

  • Occupancy optimization -- Predict arrivals/departures from Equify pipeline, suggest allocation, alert on capacity limits.
  • Condition monitoring -- Schedule periodic checks for long-custody vehicles, flag overstay, recommend actions.
  • Custody chain documentation -- Immutable log combining gate pass, inspection, photos. Directly relevant to insurance claims -- if vehicle damaged in custody, the chain proves when damage occurred.
  • Customer self-service -- Proactive updates ("Vehicle in yard 30 days. Fees: Rs X. Next inspection: [date]"). Document generation, status queries.
  • Billing automation -- Calculate charges by duration/type/services, generate invoices, track payments.

Cross-System Agent Opportunities

Agent 1: Loan Lifecycle Agent (CRM + Equify + Accounting + Inspection) -- HIGHEST PRIORITY

Tracks a deal from first customer contact to loan disbursement to first EMI collection. The connective tissue across four systems.

Customer enters CRM -> Agent checks yard for vehicle, triggers inspection, pre-checks bank docs Inspection completes -> Agent updates Equify valuation, adjusts bank recommendations, generates condition report Loan file submitted -> Agent monitors progress, drafts follow-ups, alerts sales rep Loan approved -> Agent triggers disbursement tracking, generates closing docs, schedules vehicle release First EMI due -> Agent monitors bank statement, alerts if bounced

Impact: On 100 files/month with 15-20 manual handoffs each, this eliminates 1,500-2,000 manual actions per month.

Agent 2: Risk and Compliance Agent (Equify + Inspection + Accounting + Yard)
  • Continuous portfolio health monitoring: EMI bounce rates, vehicle condition trends, payment patterns.
  • Early risk flagging: "Customer X missed EMI. Vehicle is in yard. Recommend: hold vehicle, contact customer."
  • Insurance context: generates documentation packages for insurance claims in minutes vs. days.
  • Fraud pattern detection: same vehicle in multiple files, unusual inspection-to-disbursement timing, valuation anomalies.
Agent 3: Customer Communication Agent (CRM + Equify + Yard + Gate Pass)
  • Single customer communication thread across all touchpoints.
  • Auto-generated status updates: "Loan submitted to HDFC. Expected: 7-10 days."
  • Handles routine queries without staff: "Status of my file?" "When can I pick up?"
  • Proactive reminders: EMI dates, document deadlines, appointments.
Agent 4: Yard Product Agent (Yard + Gate Pass + Inspection -- for external customers)

This IS the product differentiation for the external yard management product.

  • External customer self-service: "Park 15 vehicles this week" -> agent checks capacity, suggests timing, pre-generates gate passes.
  • Fleet analytics: "50 vehicles, avg stay 12 days, 3 condition alerts, total charges Rs X."
  • Insurance company integration: vehicle enters yard after accident -> agent documents condition, generates claim-ready report, maintains custody chain, provides insurer real-time status.
Competitive moat: The yard product's differentiation is not CRUD operations. It is the intelligence layer: predictive capacity, automated documentation, proactive communication, and insurance-grade custody tracking.

Architecture Note

All agents should share a common knowledge layer:

  • Vehicle database -- VIN/registration mapped to all records across all systems.
  • Customer database -- Customer mapped to all touchpoints, files, payments, yard visits.
  • Bank knowledge base -- Bank mapped to requirements, timelines, contacts, approval patterns.

Agent Opportunity Map

Connecting findings across all three research documents, here is where AI agents add the most value across the insurance claim lifecycle:

Pre-Claim: Prevention and Preparation

  • Insurance tracking in Equify -- Every financed vehicle needs insurance. Agent tracks policy expiry alongside loan status. "Insurance expires in 15 days, loan still active -- alert customer + rep."
  • Vehicle condition baseline -- Inspection system creates condition records at financing. If vehicle is later involved in a claim, the baseline proves pre-existing vs. new damage.
  • Yard custody documentation -- Entry/exit inspection photos + custody log serve as evidence for or against insurance claims while vehicle is in custody.

Claim Initiation (Day 0-1)

  • Guided claim filing -- Agent determines: Does this need an FIR? Which documents are required? Is this below 50K (digital survey OK) or above (physical surveyor needed)?
  • Document pre-population -- Pull policy copy, RC, DL from existing records. Auto-fill claim form. Reduce "serial document requests" pain point.
  • Towing coordination -- Connect to insurer's RSA if available; otherwise coordinate logistics.

Assessment Phase (Day 1-15)

The 50K split is the critical design decision. Below 50K, the agent can own the entire assessment: CV-based damage analysis, instant estimate, direct approval. Above 50K, the agent assists the surveyor: pre-populates report, highlights valuation mismatches, tracks the 15-day IRDAI deadline.
  • Sub-50K claims -- Full AI assessment. Smartphone photos -> CV damage analysis -> repair estimate -> approval. Digit and Acko already do this. Agent can replicate.
  • Above-50K claims -- Agent assists surveyor: pre-fills inspection data, runs CV analysis as a second opinion, flags discrepancies between surveyor estimate and market rates, tracks IRDAI timeline compliance.
  • Garage-insurer coordination -- Already electronic. Agent can automate authorization workflows, negotiate estimates against historical data, flag outlier pricing.

Processing and Settlement (Day 7-60)

  • Proactive document tracking -- Agent knows exactly which documents are missing and requests them all at once, eliminating the "serial document request" pattern that resets the clock.
  • Timeline compliance monitoring -- Track every IRDAI-mandated deadline. Alert when surveyor report is overdue. Flag when insurer decision exceeds 7-day window. Calculate penalty interest owed.
  • Depreciation transparency -- Upfront calculation of out-of-pocket costs based on parts material depreciation tables. No "depreciation shock" at settlement.
  • Dispute escalation guidance -- If settlement is unsatisfactory: agent drafts grievance to insurer GRO (citing IRDAI regulations), then IGMS complaint, then Ombudsman filing at cioins.co.in.

Long-Tail: Third-Party and Theft Claims

  • Theft claim document orchestration -- 15 documents from 5+ sources. Agent tracks which are obtained, which are pending, sends reminders, flags the NTC bottleneck.
  • MACT case tracking -- Cannot digitize the court appearance, but agent tracks case status via eCourts, alerts on hearing dates, maintains document repository, generates preparation briefs.
  • Subrogation and recovery -- Agent identifies recovery opportunities, drafts subrogation documents, tracks at-fault party's insurer response.

Cross-Cutting: Fraud Detection

  • Overlapping patterns -- Same anomaly detection that flags suspicious loan files (Risk and Compliance Agent) applies to insurance: staged accidents, inflated damage claims, suspicious timing of insurance purchase relative to claims.
  • Yard-insurance connection -- If vehicle is damaged while in custody, entry/exit photos prove when damage occurred. Prevents false claims against the yard operator.

For the Productized Yard

  • Insurance company as customer -- Insurers need yards for salvage, total loss, and investigation vehicles. An AI-powered yard with automated documentation, condition tracking, and custody chains is exactly what adjusters need.
  • Claim-ready reports on exit -- When a vehicle leaves the yard, agent generates a complete custody package: entry photos, exit photos, duration, incidents, condition delta.
