Cross-Interview Patterns

Universal findings (all interviewees agree)

  1. Silent failure is the cardinal sin. Every single interviewee identified silent failure or silent stalling as the worst agent behavior. Fail loud or don't fail at all.
  2. "I'll just do it myself" is the universal default when delegation fails. Nobody iterates with the person/tool that failed. They absorb the work.
  3. Morning debrief > real-time monitoring. Nobody wants to watch the agent work in real-time. Async summaries delivered by morning are the preferred model.
  4. Financial actions require approval gates. Hard boundary across all interviewees. No autonomous money handling.
  5. Research delegation is emotionally loaded. Multiple interviewees expressed concern about feeling "dumber" or losing serendipitous discovery. Research surfacing is acceptable; research replacement is not.
  6. Personal/creative expression is non-delegatable. Messages to loved ones, creative work, relationship-building — hard boundaries everywhere.

Product Implications Summary

The three features that satisfy every interviewee

  1. Loud failure mode. Never silently skip, stall, or fail. Report immediately with context. This is table stakes.
  2. Overnight batch mode with morning summary. Fire-and-forget at night, async debrief in the morning. The single most requested interaction model.
  3. Workflow visibility / audit trail. Show the steps the agent took. Let users inspect the process, not just the outcome. This is how trust is built.
