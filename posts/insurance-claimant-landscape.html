Insurance Claimant Landscape — Competitive Analysis

March 27, 2026 · Research

Who is building for the claimant—the person filing the claim, not the insurer? This is a landscape scan of 25+ companies across health, property, auto, disability, and life insurance. Focus: consumer-side tools and services only.

The headline numbers

73M
Americans had in-network claims denied (2023)
<1%
of denied claims are appealed
50–80%
of appeals succeed when filed

The gap is staggering: tens of millions of valid claims go uncontested every year because the process is opaque and exhausting. The companies below are betting that AI and better UX can close this gap.

Market gaps at a glance

Health Most crowded segment — AI denial appeal tools + navigation platforms dominate
Property / Casualty Biggest gap — almost no consumer tech; public adjusters haven't been disrupted
Life Insurance No consumer startups found — handled entirely by law firms
Auto / Personal Injury One notable AI player (Mighty)
Disability / Workers' Comp One standout (Atticus)

Cautionary tale

Remedy Labs (2015–2017) raised $1.9M including from Marc Benioff, built a medical bill negotiation service, then died because billing offices refused to release medical info to third parties. Lesson: data access is the critical barrier in this space.

Opportunities

  • Property/casualty consumer tech — Nearly zero competition. Public adjusters haven't been disrupted. AI could do for homeowner claims what Claimable does for health denials.
  • Life insurance claims — No consumer startups at all. Entirely handled by law firms. Ripe for a tech-first approach.
  • India: AI-powered denial appeals — ClaimBuddy's hospital model works but there's no Indian equivalent of the AI appeal letter tools. Growing insurance penetration + smartphone adoption = opportunity.
  • Cross-insurance-type platform — Every company is single-line. Nobody offers a unified "claim advocacy layer" across health, property, auto, life.
  • The appeal rate gap — 73M denials, <1% appealed, 50–80% success. This is the single biggest arbitrage in consumer finance. Anyone who can make appealing as easy as one tap wins.
  • Data access infrastructure — Remedy died on this. Whoever solves interoperable access to policy docs, medical records, and denial letters unlocks the entire space.
